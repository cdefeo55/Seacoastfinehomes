Preparing Your Home Checklist

A practical checklist I use to help sellers think through first impressions before photography, showings, and open houses.

Small improvements can change how buyers feel.

Your home only gets one chance to make a first impression. This checklist is not about making a home perfect. It is about removing distractions, creating clarity, and helping buyers notice what is strongest about the property.

Your Home’s Curb Appeal

  • Mow lawn
  • Trim shrubs
  • Edge gardens and walkways
  • Weed and mulch
  • Sweep walkways and driveway
  • Add color and fill bare spots with plantings
  • Remove mildew or moss from exterior surfaces
  • Take stains off driveway with cleanser or kitty litter
  • Stack woodpile neatly
  • Clean and repair patio and deck area
  • Remove outdoor furniture that is not in good repair
  • Make pool or spa sparkle
  • Repair broken windows and shutters
  • Clean and remove rust from window air conditioning units
  • Paint the front door and mailbox
  • Make sure doorbell is in good working order

General Interior Tips

  • Add a fresh coat of light, neutral paint
  • Shampoo carpeting or replace if necessary
  • Clean and wax hardwood floors
  • Clean kitchen and bathroom floors
  • Wash windows, vacuum blinds, and wash window sills
  • Clean fireplace
  • Clean out closets and pre-pack items you will not need before moving
  • Remove extra furniture, worn rugs, and items you do not use
  • Repair loose knobs, cracked molding, leaking taps, and squeaky doors
  • Add light scent such as potpourri or vanilla
  • Secure jewelry, cash, and valuables

The Living Room

  • Make it cozy and inviting
  • Discard chipped or worn furniture
  • Remove frayed or worn rugs

The Dining Room

  • Polish visible silver and crystal
  • Set the table to help buyers imagine entertaining

The Kitchen

  • Make sure appliances are spotless inside and out
  • Make sure all appliances are in working order
  • Clean often-forgotten spots on top of the refrigerator and under sink
  • Wax or sponge floor to a clean shine
  • Unclutter counter space
  • Organize cabinets and pre-pack what you will not need before moving

The Bathrooms

  • Remove all rust and mildew
  • Make tubs, showers, toilets, and fixtures shine
  • Make sure all fixtures are in good repair
  • Replace loose caulking or grout
  • Make lighting bright but soft

The Master Bedroom

  • Arrange furnishings to create a spacious look with defined sitting, sleeping, and dressing areas

The Garage

  • Sell, give away, or throw out unnecessary items
  • Clean oily cement floor
  • Provide strong overhead light
  • Tidy storage or work areas

The Basement

  • Sell, give away, or throw out unnecessary items
  • Create more floor space by hanging tools and placing items on shelves
  • Clean water heater and drain sediment
  • Change furnace filter
  • Make inspection access easy
  • Clean and paint concrete floors and walls
  • Provide strong overhead light

The Attic

  • Tidy by discarding or pre-packing
  • Make sure energy-saving insulation is apparent
  • Make sure the air vent is in working order
  • Provide strong overhead lighting

When It’s Time to Show

  • Make sure property profile folder and utility bills are available
  • Open draperies and shades and turn on lights
  • Pick up toys and clutter
  • Give carpets a quick vacuuming
  • Add fresh flowers where appropriate
  • Open bathroom windows for fresh air
  • Add a spicy dessert or cinnamon scent
  • Turn off television and keep music low
  • Make a fire in the fireplace if appropriate
  • Put pets in the backyard or arrange for a friend to keep them
  • Make sure pet areas are clean and odor-free
  • Make sure trash is disposed of neatly
