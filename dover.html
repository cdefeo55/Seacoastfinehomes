My perspective on Dover

How I help clients think about Dover.

Dover is one of the most interesting Seacoast markets because it gives many buyers more room to work while still offering a real downtown, restaurants, parks, and access to Portsmouth, Durham, and southern Maine. For sellers, Dover’s variety means positioning is important. A condo, an antique home, a newer subdivision property, and a waterfront-adjacent home all need to be presented differently.

Before we focus on listings, I like to talk through how you want to live. Do you want walkability, privacy, beach access, a shorter commute, a historic home, newer construction, or something that can adapt over time? Those answers matter because two homes in the same town can support very different lives.

If you are selling in Dover, I help you think about what buyers are likely to value most and how to present the home so that the right people understand it quickly.

Good questions to ask
